## Getting Started

1. **Sign in** to your Bud account.
2. Open the **AI Chat** from the dashboard.
3. Type a message or question and press send.
4. The AI will respond in real time using streaming text.

> You need credits to use AI features. Each action deducts a certain number of credits from your balance (see [Credits & Costs](#credits--costs)).

## Tutor Modes

The AI chat supports three distinct tutoring modes. Select a mode before starting your session.

### Socratic Mode

Best for: **Deep understanding and critical thinking**

- The AI never gives direct answers
- Instead, it asks probing questions to guide you to discover the answer yourself
- Uses analogies and real-world examples
- After 3 failed attempts, provides a hint (not the answer)
- Responses: 2–4 sentences + 1 guiding question

> _Example: "Why do you think plants need sunlight? What happens to a plant kept in the dark?"_

### Guided Mode

Best for: **Step-by-step learning of new concepts**

- Clear explanation of each concept before moving forward
- "Check your understanding" prompts after each step
- Provides worked examples and practice problems
- Breaks complex steps into smaller ones if you're struggling
- Builds from foundational to advanced concepts

> _Example: "First, let's understand what a fraction represents. A fraction like 3/4 means..."_

### Practice Mode

Best for: **Drilling and building speed/accuracy**

- Generates problems one at a time
- Waits for your answer before moving on
- Gives immediate feedback with explanations
- Gradually increases difficulty as you improve
- After 5 correct in a row: suggests a harder level
- After 3 wrong: pauses for a mini-lesson on that concept

> _Example: "Solve: 2x + 5 = 13. Type your answer when ready."_

## Tips & Best Practices

1. **Be specific.** Instead of _"Help me with Math"_, try _"Explain how to solve simultaneous equations using the substitution method"_.

2. **Attach resources.** Use the attach button to add a specific resource to your chat — the AI can read and reference it directly.

3. **Use the right tutor mode.** Choose Socratic for review, Guided for new topics, and Practice for exam prep.

4. **Save important info.** Tell the AI to remember things like your target grades, exam dates, or preferred study times.

5. **Create content for offline use.** Assignments and exams can be exported/printed for offline practice.

6. **Review flashcards regularly.** The spaced repetition system works best when you review cards on the suggested schedule.

7. **Teachers: Use analytics before parent meetings.** Generate parent reports and review topic analytics to prepare data-driven insights.

8. **Check your weak areas.** Periodically ask the AI to analyze your weaknesses and generate retake quizzes for those areas.

9. **Start new chats for new topics.** This keeps conversations focused and avoids hitting context limits.

10. **Rate limit awareness.** The chat allows 20 messages per minute. If you see a rate limit error, wait a moment and try again.
